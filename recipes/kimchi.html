Kimchi

The main source of information for this recipe comes from the book Wild Fermentation, wich may be found at amazon.com.

Description

Kimchi is a lacto-fermented dish from the Korean tradition. It is usually composed of a napa cabbage and Korean radish as a base, with additions like ginger, garlic, and spring oninons.

I love this kind of food. Here I will be going through a recipe I found--the source is just under the first photo--and finally learning how to make it myself. The winter is not far, and I want to stock up on pickles and such delicious fermented foods.

This dish promises to take about a week or longer to be ready for consumtion.

Ingredients

  • Sea salt
  • 1 pound/500 grams Chinese cabbage (napa or bok choi)
  • 1 daikon radish or a few red radishes
  • 1 to 2 carrots
  • 1 to 2 onions and/or leeks and/or a few scallions and/or shallots (or more!)
  • 3 to 4 cloves of garlic (or more!)
  • 3 to 4 hot red chilies (or more!), depending on how hot-peppery you like food, or any form of hot pepper, fresh, dried, or in a sauce (without chemical preservatives!)
  • 3 tablespoons/45 milliliters (or more!) fresh grate gingerroot
